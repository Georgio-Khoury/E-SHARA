Our Story

Meet the talented team behind E-S.H.A.R.A and learn about our journey.

The E-S.H.A.R.A team at Zakhem Engineering Hall, Lebanese American University

From a random afternoon brainstorming session in Zakhem Hall, the idea for E-S.H.A.R.A was born—a spark of innovation fueled by our passion for assistive technology. As engineering students at the Lebanese American University, we saw an opportunity to make a real impact by addressing the communication barriers faced by the deaf community.

E-S.H.A.R.A isn’t just a name, it carries a deeper meaning. While it stands for Electronic Sign-language Hand Augmented Recognition Assistant, it also echoes the Arabic word إشارة (Ishara), meaning "sign" or "gesture"—a perfect reflection of our mission to bridge the gap between sign language users and those unfamiliar with it.

Guided by the expertise of Dr. Samer Saab Jr, our team of four dedicated students transformed this capstone project into a movement for inclusivity. With a fusion of electrical engineering, computer science, and human-computer interaction, we set out to create a revolutionary device—one that doesn’t just translate gestures but fosters true communication and understanding.

What started as an idea in a university hall has now become our driving force: to build technology that empowers, connects, and makes the world more accessible for all.

About E-S.H.A.R.A

Bridging the communication gap between deaf and hearing communities through innovative wearable technology.

Revolutionary Gesture Translation Technology

E-S.H.A.R.A is an Electronic Sign-language Hand Augmented Recognition Assistant designed to transform how deaf and hearing individuals communicate. Our smart glove interprets sign language in real-time, eliminating barriers and fostering inclusive interaction.

Using advanced sensor technology and machine learning algorithms, E-S.H.A.R.A translates hand gestures into text and speech while also converting spoken language back into text, creating a seamless two-way communication experience.

The system supports both American Sign Language (ASL) and Arabic Sign Language (ArSL), making it accessible to diverse communities across the globe.

Key Features

Discover how E-S.H.A.R.A transforms communication through innovative technology.

Two-Way Communication

Seamlessly translates sign language to speech and speech to visual cues, enabling natural conversations between deaf and hearing individuals.

Multi-Language Support

Compatible with both American Sign Language (ASL) and Arabic Sign Language (ArSL), making it accessible to diverse communities.

Offline Functionality

Works without internet connectivity, ensuring reliable communication anywhere, anytime.

Real-Time Processing

Advanced algorithms provide instant translation with minimal latency for natural conversation flow.

Machine Learning Integration

Self-improving system that becomes more accurate over time as it learns from user interactions.

Long Battery Life

Optimized power consumption provides all-day usage without frequent recharging.

Technology Behind E-S.H.A.R.A

Explore the innovative components that power our smart translation glove.

Flex Sensors

High-precision sensors that detect finger bending and flexing, enabling accurate gesture recognition.

Force Sensitive Resistors

Capture pressure feedback for enhanced sign differentiation and detection of subtle gestures.

IMU Sensor

Tracks hand orientation and movement during dynamic gestures using accelerometer and gyroscope data.

OLED Display

High-contrast screen that shows text translations and visual feedback for speech-to-sign communication.

MEMS Microphone

Compact, high-sensitivity microphone that captures spoken input for speech recognition and translation.

Miniature Speaker

Small yet powerful speaker that outputs translated speech for seamless voice-based communication.

ESP32 dev kit v1

The esp32 transmits data from the second glove to the Raspberry pi placed on the first glove to ensure seamless communication.

How We Got Here

The step-by-step journey of bringing E-S.H.A.R.A to life

Research and Preparation

We conducted extensive research on existing sign language recognition systems and identified key limitations. We analyzed various sensor technologies and machine learning approaches to determine the optimal combination for our solution.

September 2025

Hardware Implementation

We integrated multiple sensors including flex sensors, force-sensitive resistors, and an IMU to capture hand gestures. The Raspberry Pi was configured with necessary libraries and interfaces to process the sensor data effectively.

November-March 2025

Dataset Development

We created a comprehensive dataset of sign language gestures, implementing proper timing parameters and data collection protocols. The dataset was cleaned, preprocessed, and enhanced with feature extraction techniques.

April 2025

Software Architecture Development

We developed a deep learning model by testing various network combinations and evaluating their accuracy and precision to optimize performance.

April 2025

System Validation and Refinement

We conducted rigorous end-to-end testing to confirm system functionality and measure performance metrics such as accuracy, latency, and power efficiency. Continuous refinements improved the overall reliability and user experience.

May 2025
