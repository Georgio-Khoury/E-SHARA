Our Story

Meet the talented team behind E-S.H.A.R.A and learn about our journey.

The E-S.H.A.R.A team at Zakhem Engineering Hall, Lebanese American University

As engineering students at the Lebanese American University, we were inspired to make a meaningful impact in the field of assistive technology. The idea for E-S.H.A.R.A was born when we recognized the significant communication challenges faced by the deaf community.

Our name has special significance: while E-S.H.A.R.A stands for "Electronic Sign-language Hand Augmented Recognition Assistant", it also resonates with the Arabic word "إشارة" (Ishara), which means "sign" or "gesture" - perfectly representing our mission.

Under the guidance of Dr. Samer Saab Jr, our team of four dedicated students embarked on this journey to create a revolutionary device that would bridge the gap between sign language users and those who don't understand sign language.

What started as a capstone project has evolved into a passionate mission to create technology that promotes inclusivity and accessibility for all. We've combined our diverse skills in electrical engineering, computer science, and human-computer interaction to develop a solution that makes a genuine difference in people's lives.

About E-S.H.A.R.A

Bridging the communication gap between deaf and hearing communities through innovative wearable technology.

Revolutionary Gesture Translation Technology

E-S.H.A.R.A (إشارة) is an Electronic Sign-language Hand Augmented Recognition Assistant designed to transform how deaf and hearing individuals communicate. Our smart glove interprets sign language in real-time, eliminating barriers and fostering inclusive interaction.

Using advanced sensor technology and machine learning algorithms, E-S.H.A.R.A translates hand gestures into text and speech while also converting spoken language back into visual cues, creating a seamless two-way communication experience.

The system supports both American Sign Language (ASL) and Arabic Sign Language (ArSL), making it accessible to diverse communities across the globe.

Technology Behind E-S.H.A.R.A

Explore the innovative components that power our smart translation glove.

Flex Sensors

High-precision sensors that detect finger bending and flexing, enabling accurate gesture recognition.

Force Sensitive Resistors

Capture pressure feedback for enhanced sign differentiation and detection of subtle gestures.

IMU Sensor

Tracks hand orientation and movement during dynamic gestures using accelerometer and gyroscope data.

Raspberry Pi Processor

Powerful microcomputer that runs machine learning algorithms and manages data processing and communication.

OLED Display

High-contrast screen that shows text translations and visual feedback for speech-to-sign communication.

3D Design

Exploring the physical design and ergonomics of the E-S.H.A.R.A glove

Ergonomic Form Factor

The glove design prioritizes comfort for extended wear while maintaining precise sensor placement. The 3D-printed housing protects electronic components without limiting finger movement.

Component Placement

Strategic positioning of sensors, microcontrollers, and power systems ensures balanced weight distribution and optimal signal capture for accurate gesture recognition.

How We Got Here

The step-by-step journey of bringing E-S.H.A.R.A to life

Research and Preparation

We conducted extensive research on existing sign language recognition systems and identified key limitations. We analyzed various sensor technologies and machine learning approaches to determine the optimal combination for our solution.

September 2023

Hardware Implementation

We integrated multiple sensors including flex sensors, force-sensitive resistors, and an IMU to capture hand gestures. The Raspberry Pi was configured with necessary libraries and interfaces to process the sensor data effectively.

November 2023

Dataset Development

We created a comprehensive dataset of sign language gestures, implementing proper timing parameters and data collection protocols. The dataset was cleaned, preprocessed, and enhanced with feature extraction techniques.

January 2024

Software Architecture Development

We implemented a hybrid deep learning architecture combining LSTM networks for pre-classification, FNN for static gestures, and Bi-LSTM with attention mechanisms for dynamic gestures. The system was trained and extensively tested.

March 2024

System Validation and Refinement

We conducted rigorous end-to-end testing to confirm system functionality and measure performance metrics such as accuracy, latency, and power efficiency. Continuous refinements improved the overall reliability and user experience.

April 2024
